# Lead Flow and Validation Notes

## Goal of the Landing Pages

These pages are designed to test demand, not fully sell the service yet.

The immediate goals are:

1. collect contact submissions
2. validate whether visitors are interested in the specific offer
3. compare the three offers against each other
4. follow up manually to gather deeper information
5. identify which offer seems most promising to build and sell first

## Current Conversion Model

### On-page conversion

Each landing page should ask only for a small amount of information:

- name
- email
- business name
- optional message

The goal is low friction and clean signal.

### Off-page follow-up

After contact submission, reply by email with a follow-up questionnaire or survey to learn more about:

- who the lead is
- what business they run
- what they need help with
- why they want that service
- how serious they are about paying for it

## Discount Strategy

A later follow-up can offer a discount to people who:

- complete the survey
- demonstrate genuine interest
- seem like a realistic fit for the service

The website itself does not need to mention pricing or discounts yet.

## Metrics to Track

### Primary metrics

- page visits
- contact form submissions
- contact conversion rate

### Secondary metrics

- CTA clicks
- scroll depth
- time on page
- quality of follow-up responses
- perceived seriousness of leads

## What to Evaluate After Traffic Runs

For each of the three offers, review:

- raw number of contacts
- conversion rate
- clarity of visitor intent
- quality of lead responses
- urgency of the business problem
- ease of eventually delivering the service
- likely willingness to pay

## Important Interpretation Note

The offer with the highest number of form fills is not automatically the best one.

The strongest offer may instead be the one with:

- the clearest pain
- the most serious leads
- the easiest path to delivery
- the best fit for a sustainable business

## Website Strategy Notes

- Each landing page should be one page only
- Each page should have a distinct offer and message
- Each page should remain simple and focused
- Each page should avoid unnecessary detail
- No pricing is shown yet
- Technical stack details should remain mostly backstage

## Contact UX Guidance

The pages should feel easy and low-pressure.

Good framing:

- Contact us
- Ask about setup
- Tell us what you need
- See how we can help

Avoid making the form feel like a long intake process.

## Future Workflow

1. launch the three pages
2. split traffic evenly
3. capture form submissions
4. send follow-up survey or qualifying email
5. review responses across all three offers
6. decide which business direction to pursue first
